No implementation shall overrule constitutional doctrine.

Where implementation conflicts with constitutional authority, the Constitution shall prevail.

The Constitution exists to ensure that MAESTRO remains:

* coherent;
* internally consistent;
* evidence-based;
* traceable;
* maintainable;
* commercially sustainable;
* capable of long-term evolution without architectural drift.

---

Governance defines **what** must exist.

Architecture defines **how** the governance is realised.

Implementation defines **how** the architecture is executed.

Operational procedures define **how** the implemented system is used.

Each layer shall remain distinct.

Implementation convenience shall never redefine constitutional authority.

1. Constitution
2. Constitutional Amendments
3. Governance Doctrines
4. Constitutional Policies
5. Architecture
6. Standards
7. Specifications

---

The Constitution shall preserve institutional knowledge across:

* conversations;
* implementations;
* repositories;
* contributors;
* future projects.

Loss of conversational context shall not constitute loss of constitutional authority.

---

# 9. Interpretation

Where ambiguity exists, interpretation shall favour:

* constitutional consistency;
* preservation of established doctrine;
* architectural integrity;
* evidence-based reasoning;
* explicit ratified authority.

Interpretation shall not create new constitutional authority.

---

# 10. Ratification

This doctrine establishes the constitutional foundation upon which all subsequent MAESTRO governance shall be constructed.
